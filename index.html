Adobe Caslon


The creator of this typeface is Carol Twombly. This typeface is a revival of the Caslon Typeface created by William Caslon. William Caslon was born in 1692. He is one of the most famous English typographers. He started his typographer career as an engraver. Caslon worked in London, engraving gunlocks and barrels. In 1716, Caslon began his own engraving business and soon began making tools for bookbinders. He was given the task to cut type punches for numerous presses. In 1720, he designed his first typeface, “English Arabic”, which was later used in a psalter and a New Testament. In 1722, Caslon created new roman, italic, and Hebrew typefaces for the printer William Bowyer. The roman typeface was later named after him. The Caslon typeface quickly became a favorite in England and soon replaced many of the Dutch typefaces. It was so popular it was exported throughout the British Empire and its territories, including the American Colonies. The Caslon typeface was one of Benjamin Franklin’s favorite typeface, which is one of the reasons the Caslon Old Face was used to print the first version of Declaration of Independence of the USA in 1776 .
